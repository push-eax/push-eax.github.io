About

It's me, talking about myself on the Internet.

My resume is here.

Some facts about me in descending order of professionalism

  • I'm an information security consultant.
    • Most of my work is penetration testing of applications and networks, which means I am hired by organizations to compromise the security of their computer systems by applying techniques used by hackers. After attacking my client's systems for a predetermined period of time, I write a report containing technical information about what access I was able to obtain, how, and what they need to do to prevent hackers from doing the same things I did. I perform this work through my company, Mitigon.
    • I've been doing this sort of work since 2018. My deep interest in and involvement with computers in general began circa 2005 and I got into infosec right before Kali replaced BackTrack.
    • My technical interests and expertise include application and network security, OS internals, malware analysis, social engineering techniques and their psychology, and the tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs) of modern adversaries.
    • I also come from a strong programming background and deeply enjoy writing software that solves real-world problems, usually mine. Free and open source software is important to me, and I try to release whatever I create under a permissive license.
  • I attended Framingham State University in Framingham, Massachusetts and earned my B.S. in computer science there in May 2020.
  • I really like finance and writing about finance.
    • In college, I worked for Fidelity Investments as a software engineer, and learned a lot about options trading and market behavior. Beating the market is effectively impossible without lots of data and low-latency order execution, so I don't actively trade for the purpose of growing my account, but I keep a small trading budget for fun.
    • I'm a hobbyist bookkeeper and keep my personal and business books in a double-entry fashion, initially in GnuCash. I experimented with plaintext accounting software such as beancount, which I used for about a year and a half before returning to GnuCash.
  • I like motorcycles, and am particularly fond of sportbikes.
  • Fitness, nutrition, and human physiology are deeply interesting to me. My exercise regimen includes running and weightlifting in roughly equal parts, as well as frequent hiking. I also prefer biking over driving for local transportation (where public transit is unavailable), because it's healthy, it's fun, and it's cheaper than driving.
  • My music taste is highly varied, but I especially appreciate:
    • Electronic music in its various forms
      • Most styles of drum & bass e.g. LTJ Bukem, Technical Itch, Dom and Roland, Current Value, Katharsys, Sewerslvt
      • Breakcore and other jungle derivatives, e.g. Enduser, bioxeed, Rotator
      • Ed Harrison's game soundtrack work, especially the Neotokyo Source mod OST
    • Most styles of rock, metal, and derivatives thereof e.g. Deftones, Tool, Chevelle, P.O.D.
    • Noise music e.g. Merzbow, Qebrus
    • Death Grips
  • I have ADHD. My brain has the capacity and tendency to absorb and analyze large quantities of information quickly, but controlling what it focuses on requires significant effort, which can preclude sustained focus on what I should be doing. This made my grades highly inconsistent during my entire academic career, but over the years I developed strategies to handle this. Like many people with ADHD, I also experience hyperfocus very regularly, which is focus bordering on obsession with a task or topic sustained for a long period to the exclusion of other things. This can be tremendously helpful for completing large and complex projects quickly or rapidly learning about a new topic, but it needs to be managed to avoid neglecting other urgent tasks, such as eating and sleeping; in the depths of hyperfocus I can lose awareness of my body's physical cues.
  • My mind is analytical and calculating, and I tend to think in terms of lists, functions, cost-benefit, etc. However, I also experience emotions intensely, which is probably why I'm drawn to art, as art is the primary medium for the communication of emotional states between humans, among other functions e.g. serving as a cultural lynchpin.
  • This dude was my grandfather. I come from a family of actors and I performed on stage frequently in high school, but I chose not to pursue that path professionally for various reasons.
